The Stranger Beside Me is a gripping true crime book written by Ann Rule. It tells the shocking story of the author's friendship with the notorious serial killer, Ted Bundy.

When Chloe Davis was twelve, six teenage girls went missing in her small Louisiana town. By the end of the summer, her own father had confessed to the crimes and was put away for life, leaving Chloe and the rest of her family to grapple with the truth and try to move forward while dealing with the aftermath.

The novel is about a young boy who starts hearing voices. From there, the boy Christopher gets lured into a strange world of dark magic and more power than he ever dreamed of having. So in a way it is still a coming of age book if that’s what you’re looking for.

In The Man from the Train, the authors claim to have discovered the identity and existence of a previously overlooked serial killer active in the late 1800s and early 1900s.

I'll Be Gone in the Dark is a gripping true crime book by Michelle McNamara that chronicles her relentless investigation into the notorious Golden State Killer. It offers a chilling account of his crimes and the author's obsessive quest to unmask him.
